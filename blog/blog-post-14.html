What Exactly Is a Discount?

A discount is simply a reduction in the usual price of an item or service. It's typically expressed as a percentage. For example, a $100 pair of shoes with a 20% discount means you get to subtract 20% of the original price before you pay.

Understanding discounts is key to managing your budget and knowing if you're truly getting a good deal.

How to Manually Calculate a Discount

Knowing the formula behind the savings can be empowering. It’s a straightforward two-step process. Let's break it down.

The Formula for Discount Amount

To find the amount of money you're saving, you use this formula:

Original Price × (Discount Percentage / 100) = Savings Amount

Step-by-Step Example

Let's say you want to buy a jacket that costs $150 and it's on sale for 25% off.

  • Step 1: Convert the percentage to a decimal.
    To do this, simply divide the percentage by 100. So, 25% becomes 25 / 100 = 0.25.
  • Step 2: Calculate the savings.
    Multiply the original price by the decimal. So, $150 × 0.25 = $37.50. This is the amount you save.
  • Step 3: Find the final price.
    Subtract your savings from the original price. So, $150 - $37.50 = $112.50.

The final price of the jacket is $112.50.
