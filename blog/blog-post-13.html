You've found an item for $120, but it's on sale for 15% off. You want to know the final price, but the mental math feels a bit tricky. Don't worry, we've got you covered! It’s a common question, and we’ll break it down for you.

The short answer is: 15% off of 120 is 102. The discount saves you $18.

But how do we get that number? Understanding the process is simple and can help you calculate any discount you come across while shopping. Let's walk through it.

How to Calculate 15% Off 120: The Step-by-Step Method

There are a couple of ways to figure this out. Here's the most common method, broken down into three easy steps.

  • Step 1: Convert the percentage to a decimal.
    The word "percent" literally means "per 100." So, to get the decimal version of a percentage, you just divide it by 100. In this case: 15 ÷ 100 = 0.15.
  • Step 2: Find the discount amount.
    Now, multiply the original price by the decimal you just found. This tells you how much money you'll be saving.
    $120 x 0.15 = $18
    So, the discount is $18.
  • Step 3: Calculate the final price.
    Simply subtract the discount amount ($18) from the original price ($120) to find what you'll actually pay.
    $120 - $18 = $102
    And there you have it! The final price after taking 15% off is $102.

A Faster Way to Calculate the Final Price

If you want to skip a step and find the final price directly, you can use this little trick.

If you're getting 15% off, it means you're still paying for the remaining 85% of the original price (100% - 15% = 85%).

Just convert 85% to a decimal (0.85) and multiply it by the original price:

$120 x 0.85 = $102

Both methods get you to the same correct answer!
