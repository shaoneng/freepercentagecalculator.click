# How to Calculate Percent Error: A Simple Guide

Ever followed a recipe perfectly, only to have your cake turn out slightly different than the picture? Or maybe you conducted a science experiment where your measurement was just a tiny bit off from the expected value. This difference between what you expected and what you got is common, and there’s a fantastic way to measure it: Percent Error.

Don't let the name intimidate you! Percent error is a simple and useful tool that tells you how significant your error is relative to the correct value. It's a way of saying, "How far off was I, in percentage terms?"

What is Percent Error?

Percent error (or percentage error) is a measurement of the discrepancy between an observed or experimental value and a true or accepted value. In simple terms, it’s a way to see how accurate your measurement is.

A low percent error means you were very close to the correct value, while a high percent error means your measurement was quite a bit off.

The Percent Error Formula

To find the percent error, you only need two pieces of information:

  • The Experimental Value: This is the value you measured or observed yourself.
  • The True Value: This is the real, accepted, or theoretical value.

Once you have those, you can plug them into this formula:

Percent Error = [ |Experimental Value - True Value| / True Value ] x 100

Those vertical bars | | around the first part mean "absolute value," which just means you ignore any negative sign. After all, an error is an error, whether you were over or under the true value!

How to Calculate Percent Error: A Step-by-Step Guide

Let's break that formula down into three easy steps.

Step 1: Find the Difference (The "Error")

Subtract the true value from your experimental value. Remember to take the absolute value of the result, meaning if you get a negative number, just make it positive.

Example: |Experimental Value - True Value|

Step 2: Divide by the True Value

Take the result from Step 1 and divide it by the true (accepted) value. This will give you a decimal number.

Example: Error / True Value

Step 3: Convert to a Percentage

Multiply the decimal you got in Step 2 by 100 to express your error as a percentage. Don't forget to add the percent sign (%) at the end!

Example: (Decimal from Step 2) x 100

Let's Use a Practical Example

Imagine you're trying to guess the number of jellybeans in a jar. You guess there are 310 jellybeans. Later, you count them all and find out the actual number is 325.

Let's calculate your percent error!

  • Experimental Value: 310 (your guess)
  • True Value: 325 (the actual count)
  1. Find the difference: |310 - 325| = |-15| = 15
  2. Divide by the true value: 15 / 325 = 0.046
  3. Convert to a percentage: 0.046 x 100 = 4.6%

Your percent error was only 4.6%! That's a pretty good guess.
