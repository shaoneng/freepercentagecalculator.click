# What is 66.6% as a Fraction? A Simple Guide

Have you ever come across the number 66.6% and wondered how to write it as a fraction? It looks a little intimidating, doesn't it? That repeating decimal can be tricky. But don't worry, converting it to a fraction is much easier than you think. Whether you're a student, a professional, or just someone who's curious, we've got you covered.

Let's break it down together.

The Quick Answer: What is 66.6% as a Fraction?

For those in a hurry, here's the straightforward answer:

66.6% as a fraction is 2/3.

That's it! They represent the exact same value. But how do we get there? Understanding the "why" can make dealing with percentages and fractions much less daunting in the future.

How to Convert 66.6% to a Fraction: The Step-by-Step Breakdown

The key to solving this is recognizing that the ".6" in "66.6%" is a repeating decimal (it's really 66.66666...). This repeating part is what makes it a special case.

Step 1: Understand the Repeating Decimal

First, let's look at the decimal part alone: 0.666... This is a common repeating decimal that is equivalent to the fraction 2/3. This is a great piece of math to memorize, but you can also work it out.

Because 0.666... is the same as 2/3, we can rewrite 66.6...% as 66 and 2/3 %. This makes it much easier to work with.

Step 2: Convert the Mixed Number to an Improper Fraction

Now we have a mixed number percentage: 66 2/3 %. To convert this into a single (improper) fraction, we do the following:

  • Multiply the whole number (66) by the denominator of the fraction (3): 66 * 3 = 198.
  • Add the numerator of the fraction (2) to that result: 198 + 2 = 200.
  • Place that result over the original denominator (3).

So, 66 2/3 becomes 200/3. Our percentage is now (200/3)%.

Step 3: Convert the Percentage to a Fraction

The word "percent" literally means "per hundred," or "out of 100." So, to convert any percentage to a fraction, you just put it over 100.

In our case, we take our (200/3)% and divide it by 100:

(200 / 3) / 100

Dividing by 100 is the same as multiplying by 1/100. So, the equation becomes:

(200 / 3) * (1 / 100)

Step 4: Simplify to Get the Final Answer

Now, we just multiply the fractions straight across (numerator times numerator, denominator times denominator):

  • 200 * 1 = 200
  • 3 * 100 = 300

That gives us the fraction 200/300.

Finally, we simplify the fraction by finding the largest number that divides into both 200 and 300. In this case, it's 100.

  • 200 ÷ 100 = 2
  • 300 ÷ 100 = 3

And there you have it! Our final, simplified fraction is 2/3.

Why 66.6% is Two-Thirds

Think of a pie cut into three equal slices. If you take two of those slices, you have taken two-thirds (2/3) of the pie. As a percentage, that portion is 66.6% of the whole pie. It's a common value that represents a significant majority—more than half, but not the entire thing.
