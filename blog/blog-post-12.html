What is the Percent Difference Formula? A Simple Guide

Have you ever needed to compare two numbers but weren't sure which one was the "original" value? Maybe you're comparing the price of the same item at two different stores, or the number of visitors your website got on two different days. In these cases, you're not looking for an increase or decrease, but simply the difference between them as a percentage. This is where the percent difference formula comes in handy!

It's a common point of confusion, but percent difference is not the same as percent change. Don't worry, we'll break it all down in this simple guide. We'll cover what the formula is, how to use it, and provide a clear example to make you a pro in no time.

What is Percent Difference, Exactly?

Percent difference is a measure of how different two values are, expressed as a percentage relative to their average. The key word here is average. Unlike percent change, where you have a clear "before" and "after" value, percent difference treats both values equally. Neither one is more important or serves as the starting point.

You use it when you want to know the difference between two numbers without regard to which one is older or newer. Think of it as a way to find the "middle ground" and see how far apart the two values are from there.

The Percent Difference Formula

The formula might look a little intimidating at first, but it's quite straightforward once you break it down. Here it is:

Percent Difference = [ |Value 1 - Value 2| / ((Value 1 + Value 2) / 2) ] * 100

Let's unpack that:

  • |Value 1 - Value 2|: This is the absolute difference between your two values. The vertical bars | | mean you take the absolute value, which is just the positive version of the number. So, if you get -10, you use 10.
  • ((Value 1 + Value 2) / 2): This is simply the average of the two values. You add them together and divide by 2.
  • * 100: We multiply the final result by 100 to convert it from a decimal into a percentage.

How to Calculate Percent Difference: A Step-by-Step Example

Let's put the formula into action with a practical example. Imagine you are shopping for a new pair of headphones and find them at two different online stores.

  • Store A Price (Value 1): $140
  • Store B Price (Value 2): $160

Let's find the percent difference in price.

Step 1: Find the Absolute Difference

Subtract one value from the other and take the absolute value.

|140 - 160| = |-20| = 20

The absolute difference is 20.

Step 2: Find the Average of the Two Values

Add the two values together and divide by 2.

(140 + 160) / 2 = 300 / 2 = 150

The average price is $150.

Step 3: Divide the Difference by the Average

Now, divide the result from Step 1 by the result from Step 2.

20 / 150 = 0.1333...

Step 4: Multiply by 100 to Get the Percentage

Finally, convert your decimal to a percentage.

0.1333 * 100 = 13.33%

The Answer: The percent difference between the prices at Store A and Store B is 13.33%.

Percent Difference vs. Percent Change: What's the Difference?

  • Use Percent Difference when you are comparing two values that are independent of each other. Neither value is the "original" or "reference" point (e.g., comparing the populations of two cities).
  • Use Percent Change when you are measuring how a single value has changed over time. You have a clear "old" and "new" value (e.g., tracking the change in a stock price from Monday to Tuesday).
