# 20% off 500: How to Calculate the Discount and Final Price

Shopping and see a great deal? Figuring out discounts on the fly can be tricky, but it doesn't have to be. If you've come across an item priced at $500 with a 20% off sticker, you're in the right place. Let's break it down.

The short answer is: 20% off of $500 is a discount of $100. The final price you would pay is $400.

But how do we get that number? Understanding the math is simple, and knowing how to do it can make you a much smarter shopper. We'll walk you through two easy methods to calculate it yourself.

How to Calculate 20% off 500

Here are two straightforward ways to figure out the discount and the final price.

Method 1: The Decimal Method

This is the most common way to calculate percentages. It's quick and works for any percentage problem.

  • Step 1: Convert the percentage to a decimal. To do this, simply move the decimal point two places to the left. So, 20% becomes 0.20.
  • Step 2: Multiply the original price by the decimal. This will give you the amount of the discount. In this case: $500 x 0.20 = $100.
  • Step 3: Subtract the discount from the original price. This gives you the final sale price. So: $500 - $100 = $400.

Method 2: The 10% Mental Math Trick

This is a fantastic shortcut for calculating common percentages like 20% without needing a calculator.

  • Step 1: Find 10% of the price. Finding 10% of any number is easyâ€”you just move the decimal point one place to the left. So, 10% of $500.00 is $50.0.
  • Step 2: Double it to find 20%. Since 20% is just two times 10%, you can double the amount you just found: $50 x 2 = $100. This is your discount.
  • Step 3: Subtract the discount. Just like before, take the original price and subtract your $100 discount: $500 - $100 = $400.

Let's Summarize the Savings

No matter which method you use, the result is the same. When you see that 20% off sign on a $500 item, you know:

  • Original Price: $500
  • Discount Amount: $100
  • Final Sale Price: $400
